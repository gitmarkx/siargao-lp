About Siargao

Siargao o Island presents itself as a captivating tropical haven nestled in the northeastern region of Mindanao, within the province of Surigao del Norte, Philippines. Positioned around 800 kilometers southeast of Manila, the nation's capital, it boasts a tropical climate characterized by distinct dry and wet seasons, making it a haven for surfers during the dry months from March to October. Renowned for its surf breaks, notably the famous Cloud 9, Siargao Island attracts surfing enthusiasts worldwide, offering diverse waves suitable for all skill levels amidst its reef, beach, and point breaks. Beyond surfing, the island showcases a rich cultural tapestry influenced by Filipino, Spanish, and indigenous traditions, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in local customs, dances, music, and cuisine. Noteworthy facts about Siargao include its reputation as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," its depiction in the 2017 film "Siargao," and its diverse attractions ranging from lagoons and beaches to mangrove forests and eco-tourism initiatives promoting conservation efforts.

Experience in Siargao

Siargao offers a plethora of experiences, from world-class surfing at Cloud 9 to exploring the serene beauty of the island's beaches and lagoons. You can also immerse yourself in the vibrant local culture, enjoy delicious seafood, and embark on island-hopping adventures to nearby islets like Naked Island and Guyam Island. Whether you're a thrill-seeker or seeking relaxation, Siargao has something for everyone

Cloud 9

Cloud 9 in Siargao boasts a famous right-hand reef break with powerful waves up to 10 feet high, ideal during the southwest monsoon season (September to November), attracting experienced surfers with its challenging and fast-paced conditions.

Magpupungko

Magpupungko in Siargao Island is celebrated for its breathtaking tidal pools, showcasing natural rock formations and clear waters that invite both tourists and locals to swim, snorkel, and revel in nature's beauty, especially during low tide when visitors can observe captivating marine life trapped within the poolsâ€”a definite must-visit for anyone exploring Siargao Island's wonders.

SUGBA LAGOON

Sugba Lagoon, situated on Caub Island near Siargao, presents a breathtaking expanse of blue waters, covering approximately 437 square kilometers and offering panoramic views of majestic mountains, accessible via a short day trip from General Luna for around 1500 pesos (30 USD) per person.
